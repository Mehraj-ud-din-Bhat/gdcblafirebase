Government College, Baramulla, situated at a distance of 51 Km towards north-west from the summer capital of Srinagar, is emerging as a premiere institution of higher learning in Kashmir, which over the last six decades has been imparting higher education to the people of this region. Over the years the college has produced men and women of eminence who have been contributing towards upliftment of the society in various spheres of its activities.
An English medium school, founded by the Catholic Missionaries at Baramulla, by Father Ignatius Brower and Rev. Father Simmons in 1905 and was upgraded to High School in 1913 and then gradually upgraded to the status of Intermediate College in 1938 and eventually to Degree College in 1943. Government College Baramulla was affiliated to Punjab University Lahore. It was closed on 27th Oct. 1947, re-opened on 28th June, 1954, as Intermediate college, affiliated to the then University of Jammu and Kashmir and further upgraded to Degree college in 1956. The college was subsequently taken over by the State government on the 1st of April, 1963.
Government College Baramulla attributes much of its success to the rich cultural heritage of Baramulla district in general and Baramulla town in particular. The college is located in the lap of a hillock, 4 Km ahead of main town on Srinagar-Uri National Highway road Baramulla. The high literacy rate in the town, its multi-ethnic character, tolerance and inter-community brotherhood has had its impact on the healthy growth of this institution which has proved a beacon of light for others to emulate.The college offers Bachelors general courses in Science, Humanities, and business studies.
The institution apart from the routine teaching activities has a lot to offer to its students for all round development of their personality. Students are encouraged to participate in curricular, co-curricular and extra curricular activities. Symposia, debates, discussions, seminars, dramas and competitions on different areas of social, cultural and academic importance are organized. Inter and Intra-college competitions are conducted, wherein the students are exposed to multifold cultures and ideas.Add-on-courses are available to the bonafide students in Apiculture, Data Care Management, Social work and Still Photography. Students participates in inter-house, inter-college sports activities which offer them a chance to play in inter-university sports activities. Adequate library with internet facilities are available.Hostel accomodation is available to off-station male and female students. The college is connected to EDUSAT on the national beam maintained by ISRO and state EDUSAT also.
The College offers Post-Graduate Course in Mathematics and Masters in Computer Applications affiliated to University of Kashmir.
Career Counselling Cell of the college provides literature and counselling for varoius career advancement opportunities. Under-Graduate courses in media offered by the college give the institution a mark of distinction. The courses involve project work which highlight current issues prevalent in the state.Study centres of IGNOU and MANUU (Maulana Azad National Urdu University) provide education through distance mode to students studying various under-graduate and post-graduate courses.
Students get themselves registered with NCC and NSS units for varied in-campus and out-campus activities of great educational importance.

Code of Conduct

The institution attaches top most priority to the discipline, which is a basic requisite for conducive academic atmosphere. Besides the members of College Discipline Committee, members of teaching and non-teaching staff closely monitor the behaviour of college inmates.
The students are advised to adhere to following rules during their stay in the institution:
1.   Ragging is strictly prohibited and any student indulging in this unlawful activity will have to face cancellation of his/her admission from the institution.
2.  Only the students possessing a valid Identity Card of the institution are allowed inside the campus.
3.  Students should present their selves in a formal attire while attending the institution.
4.  Cell Phones are completely banned, the students are advised not to bring any cell phone, computers, laptops or cameras to the institution.
5.  Students getting private vehicles should register their vehicle numbers with the institution.
6.  Smoking or carrying toxic substances shall invite a strict disciplinary action.
7.  No student is allowed to take law in his own hand. In case any student has a grievance, this should be addressed to in charge of the grievance cell or the Head of the institution.

The department of Botany was established with an aim to train the desirous students about understanding and practicing various aspects of plant Sciences. The course content at UG level is designed to take care of classical, contemporary and applied aspects of Botany. Alumni of the department have excelled in the fields of teaching, research and administration. Some of the important areas which are being taught at UG level are Thallophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms, Biochemistry, plant Physiology, Cell biology, Molecular biology, Plant Taxonomy, Biodiversity and Plant Ecology. The theoretical component of the curriculum is adequately supplemented by practical coursework in the laboratory as well as in the open field and in-house Botanical Museum
The department has a computer lab with adequate facilities which is put to maximum use by faculty and students. The department of Botany takes care of entire college landscaping, particularly various compulsory ingredients of the department like Green House, Botanical garden, Rock garden, Medicinal and Aromatic plants garden (MAPs) and Temperate Rosaceous Fruit Garden. Various sections have been designed within the botanical garden like shrubbery, rosary and coniferatum. The perennial shrubs have been identified and are tagged with name plated which displays their vernacular as well as botanical name. The department has more recently upgraded its herbarium which contains plant specimens of the district Baramulla and adjoining areas, by way of their proper mounting, labeling and identification. Equipped with adequate and latest agricultural tools and manpower, the department ensures that each garden/park is dotted with annuals and perennial ornamental plants which are prescribed in the UG Curriculum and which otherwise improve the aesthetic beauty and ornamentation of gardens. The faculty of the department solicits technical assistance and expertise from research institutes like Central Institute of Temperate Horticulture (CITH) Rangraeth Srinagar, Indian Institute of Integrated Medicine (IIIM) Sanatnagar Srinagar, Department of Floriculture, Govt. of J&K, SKUAST Kashmir and Kashmir University Botanical Garden (KUBG). The department also aims to inculcate entrepreneurship skill by way of providing demonstration to students about floriculture, Medicinal and Aromatic plants- their propagation and extraction of constituents oils which find application in perfumery industry.
